The FIRE (Financial Independence, Retire Early) Playbook

1. Understanding the FIRE Movement

The FIRE movement is a lifestyle and financial framework designed to maximize savings rates and build a robust investment portfolio, enabling individuals to retire decades earlier than traditional retirement ages. The core objective is gaining absolute control over your time.

  • Savings Rate: While typical financial planners recommend saving 10% to 15% of income, FIRE practitioners often target savings rates of 40% to 70%.
  • The Power of Compounding: Saving aggressively early in life allows compounding interest to take over, funding your future lifestyle completely.

2. The Mathematics of FIRE: The Rule of 25

Traditional retirement calculations rely on Bengen’s Rule of 25 derived from historical safe withdrawal rate simulations. The formula dictates that to retire safely with a 4% safe withdrawal rate, your accumulated nest egg must be exactly 25 times your annual living expenses:

FIRE Target Equation FIRE Corpus Required = Current Annual Expenses × 25

3. The Different Flavors of FIRE

Retirement is not a one-size-fits-all plan. The FIRE ecosystem has several customized strategies:

  • Lean FIRE: Designed for minimalist lifestyles. It assumes survival expenses are capped at around 75% of your standard budget.
  • Traditional FIRE: Replaces your current standard of living exactly (100% budget replacement using a 4% SWR rule).
  • Fat FIRE: Tailored for comfortable lifestyles, modeling a 125% budget to build a premium wealth cushion.
  • Coast FIRE: Hitting a milestone where your current savings, even if you never add another rupee, will grow to fund your ultimate traditional retirement goal by age 60 entirely through compounding. You only work to cover current day-to-day costs.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the 4% rule in FIRE planning?

The 4% rule suggests that if you withdraw 4% of your initial retirement nest egg in your first year of retirement, and adjust subsequent withdrawals for inflation, your capital has a highly statistically significant probability of surviving at least 30 years without total depletion.

2. How does Coast FIRE work, and what is its main benefit?

Coast FIRE is achieved when your current saved portfolio is large enough that you no longer need to save actively to secure your retirement. The primary benefit is mental freedom—you can shift to lower-stress, passion-driven, or part-time work that simply covers your current living expenses while your retirement nest egg compounds quietly in the background.
