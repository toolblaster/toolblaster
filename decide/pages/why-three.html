The Philosophy

Why only three priorities?

It sounds suspiciously simple. But limiting yourself to just three tasks per day is the ultimate antidote to modern overwhelm.

Because clarity needs limits

DECIDE limits you to three priorities per day by design. This isn’t an arbitrary rule. It’s the foundation of how DECIDE works.

The real problem isn’t doing work

Most people don’t fail because they’re lazy. They fail because they’re overwhelmed. Traditional planners and to-do lists encourage you to:

  • write everything down
  • manage endless tasks
  • constantly reprioritize

That creates decision fatigue before the day even begins. DECIDE exists to remove that.

Why not unlimited priorities?

Unlimited lists feel productive, but they usually:

  • grow endlessly
  • hide what truly matters
  • create guilt for unfinished tasks
  • get abandoned over time

When everything is important, nothing is.
Unlimited choice is not freedom — it’s friction.

Why not just one priority?

One priority is often unrealistic. Most real days include:

  • one main focus
  • one supporting responsibility
  • one personal or maintenance task

Three allows balance without overload.

Why three works

Three priorities:

  • force you to choose
  • fit within a normal day
  • reduce mental clutter
  • are easy to remember
  • feel achievable

Three is enough to make progress and small enough to stay focused.

The psychology behind the limit

When choices are limited:

  • decisions happen faster
  • confidence increases
  • follow-through improves

By asking you to choose only three, DECIDE shifts your thinking from:
“What should I work on next?”
to:
“I already decided.”

That mental relief is the real product.

The Dopamine of Done

The Rule of 3 works best when your brain is rewarded for it. When you have 20 tasks, checking off 3 feels like a drop in the bucket. When your entire focus is narrowed to just 3 goals, finishing them feels substantial. That is why DECIDE uses tactile haptic feedback and subtle visual rewards when you cross off a task. Three completed priorities feel infinitely better than seventeen unfinished ones.

What if I have more to do?

That’s normal. DECIDE doesn’t deny that life is busy. It simply asks:

Out of everything, what matters most today?

You can still use a task manager, keep long lists, or plan your week elsewhere. DECIDE just comes before those tools. It helps you choose where to start.

Why DECIDE resets every day

DECIDE resets daily because:

  • priorities change
  • yesterday doesn’t define today
  • planning too far ahead increases anxiety

DECIDE is not about controlling your future. It’s about showing up clearly today.

The Reality of Leftovers

In traditional planners, undone tasks automatically roll over, creating a snowball effect of guilt and a massive backlog by Thursday. Because "yesterday doesn't define today," DECIDE features a manual Smart Carry-Over button instead. We give you a blank slate every morning. If a missed task still matters, you can tap to carry it over. If it doesn't, let it fade away guilt-free.

This is a feature, not a limitation

DECIDE could easily allow five priorities, ten priorities, or unlimited tasks. It chooses not to — because that would break the habit. The three-priority limit protects your focus, calm, consistency, and daily return. If DECIDE ever removes this limit, it stops being DECIDE.

A simple test

If you’re unsure what to put in the three slots, ask yourself:

If I only completed these three things today, would the day still feel meaningful?

If the answer is yes, you chose well.

Common Questions

What is DECIDE?

DECIDE is a daily priority tool that helps you decide your top three priorities for today in under 60 seconds.

Is DECIDE a daily planner or a to-do list?

No. DECIDE is not a traditional planner. It does not support unlimited tasks or calendars. It is designed purely for decision clarity.

Do I need to create an account?

No. DECIDE does not require sign-up or login. Your data is stored locally on your device.

Does DECIDE work offline?

Yes. DECIDE is a local-first tool and works entirely offline once loaded.

Is my data private?

Yes. DECIDE does not upload your data to servers. Everything stays on your device.
