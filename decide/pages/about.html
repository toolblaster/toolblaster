The Story Behind DECIDE.

We built DECIDE because we were tired of feeling guilty about our to-do lists. It turns out, productivity isn't about doing more—it's about choosing better.

The Problem with Planners

The digital landscape is flooded with complex task managers, kanban boards, and infinitely scrolling lists. These tools are incredibly powerful for managing projects, but they fail at managing human attention.

When you open a traditional planner, you are immediately confronted by everything you haven't done. Instead of providing clarity, it creates a sense of dread. You end up spending more time organizing your tasks than actually completing them. We realized that what most people need in the morning isn't a complex system; they just need a quiet place to make a decision.

The Philosophy of Three

DECIDE enforces a strict constraint: you can only list three priorities.

Why only three?

Because human focus is a finite resource. If you have a list of twenty tasks, your brain naturally gravitates toward the easiest ones to get a quick hit of dopamine. You spend the whole day busy, but the needle never moves on the things that actually matter.

By limiting your choices to three, you are forced to confront the uncomfortable reality of prioritization. If you can only do three things today, what absolutely must get done? This constraint transforms a passive "to-do" list into an active declaration of intent.

Privacy by Design

In an era where every app demands an account, an email address, and a monthly subscription, we took a different route.

  • Local First: Your data never leaves your device. It is stored locally in your browser's memory.
  • No Accounts: You don't need to sign up. Just open the app and type.
  • Lightning Fast: Because there are no database calls or server rendering delays, the tool loads instantly, even offline.
